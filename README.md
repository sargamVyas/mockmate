## Problem Statement

**Problem:**
Practicing for technical and behavioral interviews out loud — not just mentally rehearsing — is one of the best ways to prepare, but most people don't have a patient human available on demand. Existing mock-interview tools are often text-only or don't adapt follow-up questions based on how you actually answered.

**What this project does:**
A voice-based mock interview application that simulates a real interviewer across three formats (behavioral, technical/LeetCode-style, and system design). Users speak their answers; the system transcribes, tracks the conversation, and asks follow-ups where relevant. At the end of the session, users receive feedback covering both content (clarity, structure, technical correctness) and delivery (confidence/emotion signals extracted from speech, drawing on speech emotion recognition research).

**Why this matters:**
Combines ~3 years of speech ML research experience (SSL-based models, cross-lingual speech emotion recognition) with software engineering experience — a way to build a real, usable tool rather than just a research prototype.
